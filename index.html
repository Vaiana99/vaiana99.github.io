Master's Research Project

Development of chitosan and dextran-based hydrogels for postoperative adhesion prevention

In this project, I developed safer, biocompatible hydrogels to prevent postoperative adhesions. I designed a new crosslinking strategy for chitosan - dextran hydrogels by replacing dextran oxidation - which can degrade the polymer and produce cytotoxic by-products, with carboxymethylation to preserve polymer integrity. Crosslinking was achieved by coupling carboxymethylated dextran (CMD) with native chitosan amines using NHS/EDC chemistry, forming stable amide bonds in aqueous media without toxic residues. Working in a research team, we optimized conditions by adding sodium bicarbonate to raise the pH of chitosan (previously dissolved in acid) and obtain it in its NH₂ form, enabling successful crosslinking. This approach creates a safer hydrogel platform that acts as a physical barrier to prevent postoperative adhesions.

Side Project : My Beer Production (small scale)

1. Malt grain crushing

This first step prepares the grains for mashing, allowing enzymes to access and convert the starches into fermentable sugars during beer production.

2. Mahsing & Saccharification

Then, crushed malted grains are mixed with hot water (mashing), and during saccharification, enzymes in the malt break down starches into fermentable sugars. This sugary liquid is called wort.

3. Wort Filtration

Wort filtration is the process of separating the liquid wort from the spent grains after mashing and saccharification.

4. Boiling and Hopping

At this step, the wort is boiled to sterilize it, and hops are added to provide bitterness, aroma, and preservation for the beer.

5. Wort Cooling

The wort is rapidly cooled to prepare it for the next step.

6. Yeast Inoculation and Fermentation

Yeast inoculation and fermentation involve adding yeast to the cooled wort, which converts sugars into alcohol and carbon dioxide, producing beer.

7. Maturation

This is the process where beer is allowed to rest after fermentation, developing its flavors, clarity, and stability before tasting.

Publications

Cofunctionalization of Macroporous Dextran Hydrogels with Adhesive Peptides and Growth Factors Enables Vascular Spheroid Sprouting

Romane Oliverio, Benoît Liberelle, Victor Patenaude, Vaiana Moreau, Elian Thomas, Nick Virgilio, Xavier Banquy, and Gregory De Crescenzo

This study aimed to create a bioactive 3D scaffold by functionalizing macroporous dextran hydrogels with RGD peptides and growth factors to support endothelial activity. This strategy enabled vascular spheroids to adhere, sprout, and effectively colonize the pores, showing that combining adhesive cues with localized growth factor delivery greatly enhances vascular development.

The role of pore size and mechanical properties on the accumulation, retention and distribution of F98 glioblastoma cells in macroporous hydrogels

Lisa Delattre, Sahar Naasri, Angela Giraldo Solano, Hélène Therriault, Simon Bergeron-Fortier, Vaiana Moreau, Benoît Liberelle, Gregory De Crescenzo, Marc-Antoine Lauzon, Nathalie Faucheux, Benoit Paquette and Nick Virgilio

This work investigated how pore size and mechanical properties of macroporous hydrogels influence the behavior of F98 glioblastoma cells. By varying hydrogel stiffness and pore dimensions, it was showed that larger pores and softer matrices promoted greater cell accumulation, retention, and homogeneous distribution within the scaffold. These findings highlight that tuning physical properties of hydrogels is critical for modeling tumor cell behavior and designing 3D platforms for glioblastoma research.

Effect of chitosan on alginate-based macroporous hydrogels for the capture of glioblastoma cancer cells

Caroline Safi, Angela Giraldo Solano, Benoit Liberelle, Hélène Therriault, Lisa Delattre, Melek Abdelkhalek, Changsheng Wang, Simon Bergeron-Fortier, Vaiana Moreau, Gregory De Crescenzo, Nathalie Faucheux, Marc-Antoine Lauzon, Benoit Paquette and Nick Virgilio

This article investigated the effect of chitosan incorporation into alginate-based macroporous hydrogels on the capture of glioblastoma cells. It was shown that hydrogels containing chitosan exhibited improved cell adhesion, accumulation, and retention compared to chitosan-free scaffolds. These results indicate that the presence of chitosan enhances the bioactivity of alginate hydrogels, making them more effective for glioblastoma cell capture in 3D culture systems.

Robust pore-resolved CFD through porous monoliths reconstructed by micro-computed tomography: From digitization to flow prediction

This study develops a computational fluid dynamics (CFD) approach for porous monoliths reconstructed via micro-computed tomography, enabling accurate flow predictions from digital models of complex pore structures.
